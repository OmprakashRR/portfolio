01 · About

Researcher & Educator

I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Built Environment Research and Innovation Centre (BERIC), Technological University Dublin, and an incoming permanent Assistant Lecturer in Construction Management.

My work focuses on energy optimisation, thermal comfort, and building resilience — developing simulation frameworks and decision-support tools that help cities and communities transition toward net-zero energy futures. I work at the intersection of building performance simulation, machine learning, and climate-adaptive design, providing data-driven solutions to the challenges of a changing climate.

I completed my Ph.D. (2024) at IIT Bombay as a recipient of the prestigious Prime Minister's Research Fellowship, awarded by the Government of India. My doctoral research produced a customisable decision-support system for community-wide net-zero planning, enabling policymakers to identify the most efficient building retrofits for achieving net-zero energy targets.

Before entering research, I spent six years at Larsen & Toubro Construction as a Design Engineer and Assistant Manager, gaining deep practical experience in mechanical systems and infrastructure project delivery.
